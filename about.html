About BAFT

Building a better future together starts with community ownership.

BAFT was born out of research highlighting how many young people in disadvantaged areas across Kirklees may struggle to buy their own home or access good quality housing, with serious knock-on effects for family stability, wellbeing, resilience and pride.

BAFT also recognises that communities are often starved of the social resources they need to become more self-sufficient and to grow.
Community self-build

More than one meaning

BAFT uses the community self-build model to address housing and social challenges. That can mean people working together on homes collectively, local groups creating low-cost housing routes, intentional communities with shared spaces, or the rejuvenation of older buildings into community assets.

Shared thread

People shaping the future together

The common thread across all of these models is communities organising together to create the kind of homes, services and places they want and need.
